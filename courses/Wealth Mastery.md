### The Core Principle: Money = Amplified Time

**Time compounds relentlessly.**  
Waste 30 minutes scrolling → you’re tired later, less productive tomorrow, smaller results this month, missed opportunities this year, a different (smaller) life in a decade. One wasted minute can never be recovered — its opportunity cost snowballs.

**Money does the exact same thing.**  
Spend $5 frivolously today → that $5 (plus what it could have earned) is gone forever. Over decades, that repeated choice creates a dramatically poorer version of you.  
Save and invest that same $5 wisely → it works while you sleep, earns returns on returns, and funds freedom later.

This is **positive vs. negative compounding** — the eighth wonder of the world (Buffett) working for or against you.

**Key Financial Truths Behind It**:
- **Compound Interest/Growth**: You earn returns on your principal *and* on previously earned returns. It snowballs.
- **Time Value of Money (TVM)**: A dollar today is worth more than a dollar tomorrow because today’s dollar can be put to work immediately (invested, earning, solving problems). Inflation erodes idle money.
- **Rule of 72** (PBD loves this): Divide 72 by your annual return rate to estimate years to double. 10% return? ~7.2 years per double. Focus on the *next* double.

Small, consistent actions today create exponential freedom later.

**Examples**:
- Invest $5,000/year at age 25 (7% average return, like broad markets) vs. starting at 35. The early starter often ends with far more despite putting in less total money — pure time advantage.
- PBD’s Doubling: $1,000 → $2,000 → $4k... 10 doubles = $1M+. Each double gets easier as the base grows.
- Buffett: Started young. Most of his wealth came later due to decades of compounding. He calls his life “a product of compound interest.”

**Engines of Positive Compounding** (Layer These):
